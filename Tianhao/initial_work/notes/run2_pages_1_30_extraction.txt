===== PAGE 17 =====
Longitudinal relaxation time (T1)
AKA spin-lattice relaxation
T1 processes re-establish a thermal 
equilibrium.
The energy from the spins goes into 
vibrations/rotations/translations of whole 
molecules, i.e. as heat.
After a 90°pulse we have Mz = 0
 M grows with T1 back to Mz = M0 :
                Mz = M0(1-e-t/T1)

Approximate T1 and T2 values at 3 T
T2 (ms)      T1 (ms)
          White matter              60             800
          Gray matter               80             1200
          CSF                           400+         2500+
 
   T2 < T1 because components at w0 and zero frequency are 
active for T2 relaxation, whereas only w0 is active for T1
      T1 gets significantly longer with increasing B0 whereas T2 values 
increase only slowly with B0.

Main MRI components
• 3 T magnet to polarize the 
subject
• 3-axis gradient coils to 
encode spatial info.
       (max. 80 mT/m)
• 123 MHz transmit RF coil 
to excite spins
       (max. ~20 μT)
• 123 MHz receive-only coil
(We use a body coil to transmit B1, separate head coil to receive.)
